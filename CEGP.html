About the Guild

A patriotic and democratic organization of student journalists

🤝 A Broad Alliance

The CEGP welcomes all publications and individuals regardless of race, class, religion, political persuasion, or language. We are united by our commitment to press freedom and the truth.

🇵🇭 Patriotic

We ardently struggle against any foreign domination and control in economy, politics, culture, and all spheres of national life.

✊ Democratic

We uphold and defend the interest and welfare of the Filipino youth and people, fighting for their right to information and expression.

📰 National Center for Campus Press Freedom

We are steadfast in protecting and defending the right of every student to seek, receive, and impart information through any medium without interference.

Our History

Over nine decades of defending press freedom

1931

Founded on July 25 through a congregation of editors from The National (NU), The Varsitarian (UST), Philippine Collegian (UP), and The Guidon (ADMU). Wenceslao Vinzons served as the first president.

1932

The guild's progressive roots emerged when leaders opposed a bill granting higher salaries to Lower House members, mobilizing campus journalists and youth.

1960s

During the rise of student activism, CEGP transformed into a union linking journalism with national issues under progressive leadership.

1972–1980s

Despite Martial Law restrictions under Ferdinand Marcos that shut down campus publications, efforts continued to reconstitute the guild until its proper reinstatement in the early 1980s.

Present Day

CEGP remains one of the strongest student organizations in the country with a network spanning hundreds of schools across 68 provinces and cities nationwide.
